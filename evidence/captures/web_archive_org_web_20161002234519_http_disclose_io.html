Disclosing a vulnerability?

Vulnerability Affecting a Software or Hardware Product

Step 1: Coordinated Disclosure directly with the vendor - In most cases, it's a good idea to alert the vendor to your discovery so they can prioritize a fix for the issue. Some vendors maintain public disclosure programs at Bugcrowd, Hackerone or Cobalt, while some have a page on their site instructing how reporters can contact them in the event of a disclosure. Others maintain a [email protected][company.com] email address. We have provided a searchable index of known reporting methods.

Step 2: Coordinated Disclosure with CERT - CERT can handle vendor coordination and disclosure process on your behalf. However, as stated on their site, they may not "coordinate or publish every report". CERT is best when you are unable to reach the vendor, do not wish for the vendor to know who you are, disagree with the vendor, or are reporting a vulnerability that affects multiple vendors. If the vulnerability you are reporting is already public, it is likely that CERT will not take any action.

Step 3: Full Disclosure. The Full Disclosure mailing list and Bugtraq mailing list often used to publicly disclose security vulnerabilities.

Had a vulnerability disclosed?

If you've recently had a vulnerability disclosed to you, consider the following:

  • Have you acknowledged the report?
  • Do you fully understand the issue?
  • Do you have contact with the reporter?
  • Are you the maintainer for the software the vulnerability affects?
  • Do you know how to fix the issue?
