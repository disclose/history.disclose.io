# Security Policy

This repository is part of **[the disclose.io Project](https://disclose.io)**, which exists to make vulnerability disclosure safe, simple, and standardized. We hold ourselves to the standard we ask of everyone else.

## Scope

This is a static content repository — a sourced history plus a self-contained HTML report (`report-site/`) served at [history.disclose.io](https://history.disclose.io). There is no application backend, no authentication, and no user data. The most likely findings here are content-integrity issues (a broken or hijacked source link, a mis-attributed date) and anything in the report site's HTML/JS.

## Reporting a vulnerability

- **Security issues:** email **hello@disclose.io**. Please do not open a public issue for a security report.
- **Broken, moved, or hijacked source links, or a sourcing/date error:** these are content-integrity issues and are best filed as a normal [correction or addition issue](https://github.com/disclose/history.disclose.io/issues/new/choose) so the fix is tracked in the open.

We aim to acknowledge reports promptly and will keep you updated as we work toward a fix.

## Safe harbor

The disclose.io Project supports good-faith security research. When you make a good-faith effort to comply with this policy during your research, we will consider your research authorized, we will work with you to understand and resolve the issue quickly, and we will not recommend or pursue legal action related to your research.

For the canonical, community-maintained safe-harbor language — the terms whose history this very repository documents — see **[dioterms](https://github.com/disclose/dioterms)**.
